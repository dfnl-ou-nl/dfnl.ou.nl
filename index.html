Mission & Vision

The DFNL Doctorate Seminar is a recurring online seminar for researchers to present and discuss recent advances in digital forensics. The seminar is aimed at early-stage researchers and PhD candidates affiliated with Dutch universities and organizations, but it is open to everyone interested in digital forensics.

The seminar covers all academic aspects related to digital investigations. These include, but are not limited to, technical methods (data acquisition, data interpretation, AI), legal and ethical issues (privacy, admissibility, authenticity), and foundational theory of digital forensics.

Seminar Format

Our online sessions are hosted every four weeks via Microsoft Teams, provided by the Open Universiteit. Each 30-minute block is optimized for impact:

  • 20 Minutes: Expert presentation on novel or published research.
  • 10 Minutes: Collaborative Q&A and networking.

Research Scope

We explore a diverse range of topics essential to modern digital investigations:

  • Technical Methods: Data acquisition, mining, and AI implementation.
  • Legal & Ethics: Privacy, data admissibility, and authenticity.
  • Foundations: Theoretical models and conceptual frameworks.

Our Community

This initiative is primarily tailored for PhD candidates and early-career academics at Dutch research institutions. We foster cross-institutional collaboration to strengthen the national forensic landscape.

Registration

We distribute the login information for each session via a mailing list. To register to the mainlining list, send an email to dfnl(at)ou.nl with the subject “Registration DFNL”. You will receive a confirmation when you have been successfully added to the mailing list.

If you want to unsubscribe from the mailing list, send email to dfnl(at)ou.nl with the subject “Unregistration DFNL”. We will then delete your information and and confirm once your details have been removed.
